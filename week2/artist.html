Biography

Aespa is a South Korean girl group formed by SM Entertainment, debuting on November 17, 2020. The group consists of four members: Karina, Giselle, Winter, and Ningning. Aespa is notable for incorporating a metaverse concept, where each member has a digital avatar, blending reality and virtuality. Their debut single "Black Mamba" set a record for the most views for a K-pop debut music video in 24 hours. They gained wide recognition with singles like "Next Level" and "Savage," which achieved commercial success and critical acclaim. Aespa's music fuses genres such as hip-hop, EDM, R&B, and pop with futuristic soundscapes. Their first EP, "Savage," became the highest-charting debut album by a K-pop girl group on the US Billboard 200 at number 20. Their second EP, "Girls," broke records as the best-selling album by a K-pop girl group and sold one million copies in its first week, debuting at number three on the Billboard 200. Their third EP "My World," released in 2023, sold over two million copies and was another top-ten Billboard album. In 2024, their first full studio album "Armageddon" featured the lead single "Supernova," which topped the Circle Digital Chart for eleven weeks. Their music and innovative concepts have earned them prestigious awards, including Song of the Year at major Korean music awards. Aespa stands out in the K-pop industry by popularizing the hyperpop genre and for their futuristic, cutting-edge visuals and sound. They have made significant global impacts, including historic performances like Coachella in 2022. Their integration of digital avatars creates a unique storytelling experience in their music and performances, capturing a wide international fanbase while pushing the boundaries of K-pop culture.
